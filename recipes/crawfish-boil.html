Crawfish Boil

Crawfish Boils are wonderful messy affairs that are best suited for the outdoors.
Boiling crawfish is a festive event and eating is also thirsty work, so we made sure to have lots of beer on hand.


Ingredients

  • One 35-40 lb. sack of live crawfish
  • 4 lbs. new potatoes
  • 4.5 lb. bag of Louisiana Powdered Fish Fry Products Crawfish Shrimp and Crab Boil
  • oz Louisiana Fish Fry Products Liquid Crab Boil (optional)
  • 4 oz Louisiana Fish Fry Products Cayenne Pepper (optional)
  • 2 lbs. of quartered onions
  • 5 garlic pods
  • 1 lb. quartered lemons
  • 10-15 corn cobbettes
  • Two (3 oz) bags of Louisiana Fish Fry Products Crawfish Shrimp & Crab Seed Boil (optional)

Instructions

  1. Wash LIVE crawfish in a #3 Tub with fresh water until runoff is clear
  2. Remove any dead crawfish
  3. Save crawfish sack for potatoes
  4. Add two 3 oz. bags of Louisiana Fish Fry Products Crawfish, Shrimp & Crab Boil to pot
  5. Add live crawfish, onions, garlic, lemons, and corn to your boil basket and carefully lower into boiling water
  6. Turn your burner to high and return to a boil
  7. BOIL FOR 5 MINUTES AFTER WATER RESUMES BOILING
  8. Turn off burner
  9. Let your crawfish and potatoes soak 20 minutes
  10. Taste your crawfish. If you would like them spicier, let them soak another 5 minutes
  11. Remove from pot and set aside in container.
